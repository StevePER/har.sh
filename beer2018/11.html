The Beer

First brewed in Manchester in 1971, Boddingtons is notable as one of the first beers to be packaged in cans containing a widget. In the UK it is typically served at 5 to 7°C, though in some locations it is served “extra cold” at 3 to 5°.

Boddingtons has a distinctive straw-golden coloured body with a creamy white head, which is achieved by the addition of nitrogen. A higher alcohol version, “Boddingtons Pub Ale” is also available in deceptively similar packaging.

The Brewery

Boddingtons was originally brewed at Strangeways Brewery, Manchester, which was founded in 1778. Henry Boddington joined the company in 1832 and took over the brewery in 1853. The Boddington family retained partial ownership of the brand for over 130 years, until 1989.

Sales of Boddingtons beer peaked in 1997 but then declined significantly after 2000 under the ownership of Interbrew (later known as InBev, and then AB-InBev).
