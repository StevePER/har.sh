The Beer

The White Ale story starts in medieval times with brewers' mind stretching flavour experimentation . . . Belgian monks played with flavour enhancing ingredients and spices such as wheat, coriander, orange peel and other oddball additions and suspended yeast in their ales to create a cloudy white appearance . . .

With our minds dreaming of beer loving monks from days gone by and our intrigue of speciality Belgian yeast strains we couldn't resist the idea of tackling the creation of a great Australian White Ale.

White Rabbit White Ale celebrates the tradition of brewing with imagination and creativity and delivers refreshing hints of coriander, juniper berry and bitter orange, blended with a hefty dose of unmalted wheat. Light citrus aromas round out a classic, cloudy white ale with just a gentle hint of bitterness.

Imagine Belgians snuggling out of the cold in wonderful little ale houses dreaming of places like Australia to drink such a beer.

The Brewery

Beginning life in the leafy surrounds of Healesville, Victoria — White Rabbit Brewery was born to be different. Occupying an imaginative space in the Yarra Valley, the brewery made use of the original brew kit from our friends at Little Creatures plus two large, open top fermenters. As our imagination swelled, so too did our production. We required more space to play and grow, so we packed up our kit and set off for a new adventure in Geelong.

Beer Fact of the Day

The Wat Pa Maha Chedi Kaew temple in Thailand was constructed with 1.5 million empty Heineken and Chang beer bottles. Collection of the bottles began in 1984; it took two years to build the main temple. Thereafter, the monks continued to expand the site, and by 2009 some 20 buildings had been similarly constructed.
