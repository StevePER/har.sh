The Beer

Wychwood Goliath is traditionally craft-brewed with Pale and Crystal Malts for a sturdy ruby colour and rich malty taste. With a hefty whack of English Fuggles and Styrian Goldings hops for a classic refreshing bitterness. The beer to slay a mighty thirst!

Wychwood Goliath has a clear copper body which delivers a subtle, nutty yet buttery aroma with a delicate carbonation. The caramelised taste tops off this traditionally brewed beer perfectly.

The Brewery

Founded in 1983 as Glenny Brewery, but after a change of ownership the company was renamed Wychwood Brewery in 1990, taking its name from the ancient Wychwood Forest. The artwork on the brewery's labels is inspired by the myths and legends of the forest. Wychwood brews 8.2 million litres of cask ale each year and is the UK's largest brewer of organic ales.

In 2010, British Prime Minister David Cameron presented Barack Obama with a gift of twelve bottles of Wychwood's Hobgoblin ale. Obama remarked that he would drink the beer chilled, prompting Wychwood to create a shirt asking “What's the matter Obama, afraid you might taste something?”

Beer Fact of the Day

All beers have a best before date of one year after the date of production, regardless of the style of beer or alcohol content. Generally the heavier the style of beer, the longer it should last.
