The Beer

Schöfferhofer Hefeweizen Naturtrüb is the most popular variant in the classic Schöfferhofer range. It still has a bit of yeast in the bottle and the increased addition of hops it tastes a bit bitter, at the same time very fresh and fruity-spicy. And visually, the naturally cloudy Hefeweizen has much to offer. It shimmers amber in the glass and is thus visually a pleasure.

Enjoy the special tingle - with Schöfferhofer Hefeweizen Naturtrüb!

The Brewery

I found hardly anything about this brewery! Maybe all the information is in German.

Schöfferhofer is named for the former house of Peter Schöffer (a pioneer of the printing industry). The brewery was founded in the house known as Mainzer Schöfferhof. The beer sports a portrait of Peter Schöffer as its trademark. The Schöfferhofer brand originates from this brewery in Mainz, which is also known as the Brauerei Dreikönigshof.

Beer Fact of the Day

The first documented use of hops in beer is from the 9th century, though Hildegard of Bingen, 300 years later, is often cited as the earliest documented source. Before this period, brewers used a “gruit”, composed of a wide variety of bitter herbs and flowers, including dandelion, burdock root, marigold, horehound, ground ivy, and heather.
